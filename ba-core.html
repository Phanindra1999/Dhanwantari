English Literature

In this course, you will study the history, structure, and themes of English literature, including works by famous writers like Shakespeare, Jane Austen, and Charles Dickens.

History

This course explores the development of human civilization over time, from ancient civilizations to the modern era, with a focus on major historical events and the social, cultural, and political forces that shaped them.

Philosophy

This course examines fundamental questions about the nature of reality, knowledge, ethics, and the meaning of life, drawing on the writings of famous philosophers like Plato, Aristotle, and Kant.

Psychology

In this course, you will learn about the scientific study of the human mind and behavior, including topics such as perception, cognition, memory, emotion, motivation, and social influence.

Sociology

This course explores the social structures and dynamics that shape human behavior, including topics such as culture, inequality, race, gender, and social change.

Political Science

This course examines the systems and processes of government, including topics such as political theory, democracy, international relations, and public policy.

Anthropology

In this course, you will learn about human cultures, societies, and civilizations, with a focus on the study of human diversity, history, and evolution.

Economics

This course explores the principles of production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services, including topics such as microeconomics, macroeconomics, and international trade.

Fine Arts

This course introduces students to various forms of visual and performing arts, including painting, sculpture, music, dance, and theater, with a focus on the history, theory, and practice of each discipline.

Linguistics

This course examines the nature of language and its role in human communication, including topics such as phonetics, syntax, semantics, and pragmatics.
